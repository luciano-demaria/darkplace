I recall something Roland Barthes wrote about how insignificant details allow us to gain a feeling of "being there" in whatever is being
described. I think of movies, television shows, and video games, and how insignificance typically presents itself through images
rather than text in these mediums. When examining books, however, the writer must rely solely on imagery in written descriptions...
A hybrid of book and game, a text-based game, relies on imagery in its writing and immense thoughtfulness in its employment of sound.
