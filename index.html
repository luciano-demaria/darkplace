The final webpage will be a text-based game based on the game "Welcome to the Dark Place" by Zeekerss. I'm currently
troubleshooting the navigation system; I had it working not long ago. Above is an example of what the final structure
and citations might look like.

I wake up on the red carpeted floor of a library. There are some shelves ahead of me and an open door
off to the right. A warm breeze drifts in from behind me, where I notice an open door leading outside.



Upon moving closer, I realize that the wooden shelves are actually made of concrete. The aisles appear to be ordered alphabetically.



The note reads like the beginning of an essay:

"Welcome to the Dark Place is a case study in the buildup of tension through impeding the player's
senses. With little to see other than a black screen and some text descriptions, deep listening is one of
the only methods by which the player is allowed to percieve the game world."


On the back of the note, more is written in tiny scrawls:

"Ernest H. Lindley writes: 'The animals most gifted with experimenting techniques, those which utilize changes
in environment most promptly, will be favored in the struggle for existence.'[ 1 ] The Dark Place is dreamlike, and
it does not function in the same way that our reality functions. It serves as an unexplored environment for
us to adapt to. Of course, adaptation often manifests as the result of failure-"

The handwriting becomes less and less legible until it becomes chicken scratch.


1. Lindley, Ernest H. “A Study of Puzzles with Special Reference to the Psychology of Mental Adaptation.” The American Journal of Psychology 8, no. 4 (1897): 431–93. https://doi.org/10.2307/1411772.
